THE FOUNDATION

For centuries, Muslim scholars preserved and refined the art of horary astrology — the practice of consulting the heavens for precise answers to singular, burning questions. Works like the legendary Shams al-Ma'arif documented celestial correspondences, the motion of stars, and the deep connections between cosmic moments and human destiny. Scholars including Ahmad al-Buni and Al-Kindi studied the relationship between time, the heavens, and human affairs with rigorous precision. Shams al-Asrār is built on this legacy.

THE NAME

Shams al-Asrār means "Sun of Secrets." In the horary tradition, the sun is the supreme governor of time. Every hour of every day is ruled by the sun's motion through the heavens. Your question arrives in a specific moment — precise to the second. In that moment, the sun occupies an exact position in the sky. Five celestial powers converge at the instant of your question. When these forces align, the truth is certain. When they scatter, the answer grows complex — but it is still true. Like the sun revealing what darkness conceals, Shams al-Asrār illuminates what remains hidden in your situation.
